Aurora Marco. Presentation

Who am I?

Hello, my name is Aurora Marco. I studied Chemistry, Biochemistry and in Food Science and Technology in the University of Valencia. I completed my PhD at the Institute of Agrochemistry and Food Technology of the Spanish Spanish National Research Council  (CSIC).

Since 2018, I have been working as a Physics and Chemistry teacher at IES Enric Soler i Godes in Benifaió. I am passionate about integrating digital tools into education and promoting active learning methodologies in the classroom.

My school


My school is a small institution that was built in 1983 to accommodate 300 students, but it now serves over 500. We offer compulsory secondary education (ESO), starting with five classes and ending with two, as well as baccalaureate programs and learning improvement initiatives. Additionally, we provide basic, intermediate, and advanced computer training courses. Unfortunately, there have been no improvements to the infrastructure since the school was established. For nearly 20 years, we have relied on temporary classrooms, known as 'barracks,' and until 2023, we did not have Wi-Fi installed. Our school also lacks an elevator, and we have only two computer rooms available for all ESO groups. Recently, laboratories and departmental spaces have been converted into classrooms.

My Team

In the shool, we have a driving team committed to and passionate about teaching. This group is characterized by its constant collaboration, where each member contributes their experience and knowledge to enrich the learning environment. Together, we share teaching strategies and resources, allowing us to enhance our students' educational experience. Open communication and mutual support are fundamental in our daily work, helping us tackle the challenges that arise. We strive to create an inclusive and motivating environment that promotes the overall development of our students.
