Mentored Projects

Consciously

Technovation Girls 2020

In this project, students learned how to program a mobile app from scratch to calculate the carbon footprint of everyday activities, such as food consumption, transportation, and the use of household appliances. Despite having no prior experience in programming, neither the students nor I, we successfully developed the app. This initiative not only introduced the basics of coding but also aimed to raise awareness about sustainable living by allowing users to track and reduce their carbon emissions based on their daily habits.

Daiment

Technovation Girls (2021)

In this project, students with no prior programming experience worked on creating a mobile app designed to support individuals with Alzheimer's and their caregivers. The app included features such as GPS tracking for location, a calendar and alarm system for medication reminders, a personal information page in case the person got lost, photo galleries to help with memory, and memory games to stimulate cognitive function. Additionally, the app incorporated a fall detection system that would automatically contact emergency services, providing an essential safety feature for those at risk.

Unveiling the Cosmic Secrets with the Cloud Chamber

Fira experimenta 2024

Project where three 3rd-year ESO students attempted to build a cloud chamber to observe alpha particles and other types of radiation. Through this project, they explored fundamental concepts in nuclear physics, experimenting with radiation detection and particle observation, while also developing practical skills in constructing scientific equipment. Their goal was to better understand cosmic particles and radioactive phenomena, making complex physics tangible and engaging.

Save the penguins

Fira experimenta 2024

In this project, 2nd ESO students studied heat transfer mechanisms and insulating materials with the goal of building shelters for penguins. At the same time, they aimed to raise awareness about the importance of improving thermal insulation in homes to increase energy efficiency. Through this activity, they combined scientific experimentation with reflection on environmental impact, linking physics to real-world challenges such as energy savings and sustainability.
